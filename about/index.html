About.

Hello!

I’m Corey, a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the University of British Columbia (UBC) in beautiful Vancouver. I’m based at the Biodiversity Research Centre in Prof. Patrick Keeling’s Lab where I study microbial diversity and evolution. I’m currently working with the Tula Foundation’s Hakai Institute and the Sentinels of Change initiative. I’m using settlement plates, light traps, and plankton tows to assess symbiont diversity affecting invertebrate recruitment in relation to environmental change.

I’m interested in all things microbial diversity, particularly how and why microbes live in particular hosts or environments. These communities of microbes (i.e., microbiomes) can be integral to host and/or ecosystem functioning and I want to better understand how microbes fit into the web of processes that govern the marine environment.
